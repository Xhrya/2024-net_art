The works that I have chosen to include in this exhibition are "The Thing" by Wolfgang Staehle, "Conversation Map" by Warren Sack, "ART DIRT IM-PORT" by G.H. Hovagimyan and guests, and ".ping Project" by Umstätter, Antya; Meschkat, Steffen; Schmidt, Axel. All play around with the idea of the internet bringing people together in a way that creates a sense of information overload and an interwoven web of information. Most notably seen through "The Conversation Map", which reflects on a very large scale, the usage of the internet - and the interconnected web and relationships between messages and their results, and the overwhelming amount of lines connecting the different conversations are seen. This, followed by the ".ping project", further digs deeper into the visible structure of the network connection. I find it interesting that the interactive map of the World Wide Web, as shown through the visual camera in this ".ping" computer program, again shows a series of lines (which create a web) that are interconnected in a way that’s hard to untangle. The of lines forming the web, once again, highlights the interactive nature of the internet, and the mass amount of data it contains. Next, I feature two artworks that reflect on how conversations and data are added to the web as seen in the first two artworks. "The Thing" acted as a means to further the realm of data nets and communications. Here, various discussions and conversations could be had. If looking at it as a larger picture, these conversations and strings contributed to the web as seen in the ".ping", and "Conversation Map". The final artwork that I decided to include, "ARTDIRT IM-PORT", is an ironic representation of the bundled strings as seen in the other two artworks. I found this piece interesting because although the first two pieces of artwork showcase an overcrowded ball of information, here, through a chat window that shows a two-way conversation between port1 and port2. Within the confines of their physical spaces, these two are lonely, as shown through the various screenshots - covering their bodies with a blanket and isolating themselves in front of the computer, or lying on the floor with their faces veiled - giving off the feeling of isolation. Despite this, the content of their conversation reflects the mass amount of information available through the internet: “more movies…”, and overall, lots of data. All in all, I feel that these artworks tie together the idea of interconnectedness and the complex interleaved web of the excessive amount of information on the web. These artworks make me delve deeper into the complexity, nature, and implications of having a mass amount of data about ourselves on the digital web, and how this data ties together into a bigger picture.
