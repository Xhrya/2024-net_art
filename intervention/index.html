Exploring the Dynamics of Friendship on Depop

About the platform

Depop, primarily recognized as a platform for buying and selling fashion and second-hand goods, captivates me due to its unique blend of commerce and community. The platform not only serves as a marketplace but has also evolved into a social space, especially among younger generations, blurring the lines between shopping and social interaction.

Intervention Timing

The intervention happened on Depop with the posting of a rather peculiar item for sale - "Friendship." Posted on Thursday, March 7th, the objective was to challenge the typical product listings and initiate conversations about loneliness, especially in the post-COVID context. The timing aimed to prompt users to reflect on the broader societal implications of commodifying human connections.

Effects

The initial time span of the intervention yielded limited responses, with no immediate engagement or replies. This outcome was unexpected, yet expected at the same time. The lack of immediate response seems to be a byproduct of the limited exposure that the listing might have received. With an array of items flooding the app, it's possible that this listing as well, got flooded in the noise.

Future thoughts:

Given additional time, I would proactively engage in the comments section, sparking conversations and responding to any queries or comments. Furthermore, I would explore cross-platform promotion, sharing the intervention on other social media channels to expand its reach. Collaborating with influencers or users with a significant following on Depop could amplify the visibility of the listing and potentially generate more engagement. In reflection, the intervention's effectiveness remains an ongoing process, and adjustments may be needed to enhance visibility. The experience provides valuable insights into the challenges of introducing unconventional concepts in an online marketplace.
